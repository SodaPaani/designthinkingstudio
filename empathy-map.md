# Empathy Map
A simple tool I use to understand users better before designing solutions. This is my interpretation and how I apply it in learning design.

## What this is
Empathy maps help you understand the user you are designing for their challenges and context, so you don’t design in isolation.

<img width="1400" height="906" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/21093073-21a6-4f6f-a552-3e4b47c74c6a" />
What I value most about this tool is its simplicity - it encourages designers to step into the user’s perspective, something we often skip due to time pressures.

### Why it matters
Research is a critical part of the design process — it ensures we are solving the right problem, not just producing solutions in isolation.

## My take
This tool is powerful because it forces a shift from assumptions to perspective. In fast-paced environments, we often skip this step, which leads to solutions that miss the real problem.

## Important
Empathy maps are useful, but they should be supported by other research methods such as interviews or observations.

## When I use this
I use empathy maps at the start of a project to avoid designing based on assumptions rather than real user needs.
