# Empathy Map
A simple tool I use to understand users better before designing solutions. This is my interpretation and how I apply it in learning design.

## What this is
Empathy maps are a really useful template that guides you in getting to know the end user you are designing for - their pain points and what their challenges are based on your observations in their environment, so you can take it into consideration when designing

<img width="1400" height="906" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/21093073-21a6-4f6f-a552-3e4b47c74c6a" />
The simplicity and clarity of this tool are most effective for me - I love the way it invites thinking that encourages the designer to consider a different perspective, a user perspective we don’t generally have time for.

### Why it matters
Research is a key part of the design process as it helps us understand what we are solving for rather than create in isolation.

## Notes
Empathy maps are great, but they should be validated with other research methods, e.g., interviews or observations.
