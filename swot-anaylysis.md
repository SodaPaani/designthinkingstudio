# SWOT Analysis

A simple tool I use to evaluate a situation by looking at internal strengths and weaknesses, and external opportunities and threats.

## What this is

SWOT analysis helps break down a problem or situation into four areas — strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats — to support clearer decision-making.

## When I use this

I use this when I need a quick, structured way to assess a situation, especially during early scoping or when comparing options.

## How it works

- **Strengths**: What is working well internally?
- **Weaknesses**: What is limiting progress internally?
- **Opportunities**: What external factors could be leveraged?
- **Threats**: What external risks or constraints exist?

## My take

SWOT is simple and widely used, but its value depends on how honestly it is applied. Without critical thinking, it can become a superficial exercise.

## Important

SWOT should be used as a starting point, not the final analysis. It works best when followed by deeper investigation or prioritisation.
